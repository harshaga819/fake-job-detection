Recognize the Red Flags

Knowledge is your best defense. Learn to spot the common characteristics of fraudulent job postings to keep your job search safe and secure.

Unrealistic Salary

Offers that seem too good to be true, promising high pay for little work or experience.

₹50,000/week for data entry Earn ₹10,000/hour

High Urgency

Pressure to accept an offer immediately without giving you time to think or research.

Offer expires today! Only 2 spots left

Vague Descriptions

Lack of specific details about the job responsibilities, company, or necessary qualifications.

Work from home No skills needed

Unprofessional Communication

Use of generic email addresses (like @gmail.com) instead of a corporate domain, plus poor grammar and spelling.

hr.recruiter@gmail.com Multiple typos

No Interview

Offering a job without a formal interview process, or conducting interviews through text/chat apps.

Instant hiring Chat-based interview

Upfront Payment

Requesting payment for training materials, background checks, or equipment. Legitimate employers never ask for money.

Pay for starter kit Training fee required

How to Protect Yourself

Research the company thoroughly using official websites and reviews

Verify contact information and physical address

Be skeptical of jobs that seem too good to be true

Never provide personal financial information upfront

Trust your instincts - if something feels wrong, it probably is

Use legitimate job boards and company career pages

Ask detailed questions about the role and company during interviews
